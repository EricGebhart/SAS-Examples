Arkas, my Maremma-Abruzzes Puppy
This is Arkas my 7 month old maremma sheepdog. He had a rough start in life. At 7 days old all of his siblings had died and his mother was very sick. Arkas nearly died in that second week. But as you can see he is doing fine now. Maremma sheepdogs are from the Maremma and Abruzzese regions of Italy. The breed's full name is Pastore Il Maremma-Abruzzese. The Maremma is related to the few other big white dogs of the world.The Maremma is most similar to the Great Pyrenees, and Kuvaz. Maremma's supposedly top out at 100 lbs.But there is a lot of variation since the breed standard is really about two different dogs, the one from Maremma, and the one from Abruzzese. The dogs from Abruzzes can be very big, up to 100 kg. All of the BWD's have the same job, guarding livestock. The dogs are bonded to the livestock and live with them full time. They don't herd so much as lead and guard. They are very easy going and somewhat lazy in their apparant behavior.
Arkas' Growth
It's hard to believe how small Arkas was. Or how fast he grew...Here's a picture from his second week. The picture under the chart is from his 22nd week when he only weighed 83 lbs.

Later the weekly trip to the vet was just to keep him happy about going to to the vet. He has grown really fast but he seems to be slowing down a bit. He is already bigger than his his father, and what the breed standard says. He won't be full grown until 2 years of age so he has a ways to go.
